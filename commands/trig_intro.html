INTRODUCTION TO TRIGGERS

Definition: A Trigger in PL/SQL is a stored program that is automatically executed or fired when certain events occur on a particular table or view. These events can be INSERT, UPDATE, or DELETE.

Why Triggers?

  • To enforce complex business rules automatically.
  • To track changes in table values (auditing).
  • To perform validation before or after data modification.
  • To automatically generate values or logs.

Types of Triggers:

  • Before Trigger – Executes before the triggering DML event.
  • After Trigger – Executes after the triggering DML event.
  • Row-level Trigger – Executes for each row affected.
  • Statement-level Trigger – Executes once for the triggering event.

Basic Syntax:

CREATE OR REPLACE TRIGGER trigger_name
BEFORE | AFTER INSERT | UPDATE | DELETE
ON table_name
[FOR EACH ROW]
BEGIN
  -- Trigger logic here
END;

Note: Triggers do not accept parameters and are automatically executed when the specified condition is met.
