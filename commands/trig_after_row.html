AFTER ROW LEVEL TRIGGER

Definition: An AFTER ROW LEVEL TRIGGER is a type of trigger that fires automatically after each row is inserted, updated, or deleted from a table. It's commonly used for logging changes or updating other tables after data modification.

Syntax:

CREATE OR REPLACE TRIGGER trigger_name  
AFTER INSERT OR UPDATE OR DELETE  
ON table_name  
FOR EACH ROW  
BEGIN  
   -- trigger logic here  
END;

Example:

This trigger inserts a log entry after a new employee is added to the employees table.

CREATE OR REPLACE TRIGGER log_insert  
AFTER INSERT ON employees  
FOR EACH ROW  
BEGIN  
  INSERT INTO emp_log(emp_name, log_date)  
  VALUES (:NEW.name, SYSDATE);  
END;

Explanation:

  • :NEW refers to the new row being inserted.
  • The trigger fires after the insert is completed.
  • Used for auditing, logging, or further actions after a change.
