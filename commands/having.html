HAVING CLAUSE

Definition:The HAVING clause is used to filter groups of records created by the GROUP BY clause. It is like the WHERE clause, but HAVING is used with groups/aggregates (not individual rows).

Syntax:

SELECT column_name, AGGREGATE_FUNCTION(column_name) FROM table_name GROUP BY column_name HAVING condition;

Example:

SELECT department, COUNT(*) FROM employees GROUP BY department HAVING COUNT(*) > 5;

Output:

mysql> SELECT Age, COUNT(*) 'Total Student' FROM student_data GROUP BY Age HAVING Age > 19;
+------+---------------+
| Age  | Total Student |
+------+---------------+
|   21 |             3 |
+------+---------------+
1 row in set (0.07 sec)
